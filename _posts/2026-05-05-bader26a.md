title: 'MApLe: Multi-instance Alignment of Diagnostic Reports and Large Medical Images'
abstract: In diagnostic reports, experts encode complex imaging data into clinically
  actionable information. They describe subtle pathological findings that are meaningful
  in their anatomical context. Reports follow relatively consistent structures, expressing
  diagnostic information with few words that are often associated with tiny but consequential
  image observations. Standard vision language models struggle to identify the associations
  between these informative text components and small locations in the images. Here,
  we propose "MApLe", a multi-task, multi-instance vision language alignment approach
  that overcomes these limitations. It disentangles the concepts of anatomical region
  and diagnostic finding, and links local image information to sentences in a patch-wise
  approach. Our method consists of a text embedding trained to capture anatomical
  and diagnostic concepts in sentences, a patch-wise image encoder conditioned on
  anatomical structures, and a multi-instance alignment of these representations.
  We demonstrate that MApLe can successfully align different image regions and multiple
  diagnostic findings in free-text reports. We show that our model improves the alignment
  performance compared to state-of-the-art baseline models when evaluated on several
  downstream tasks.
