title: 'StainNet: Scaling Self-Supervised Foundation Models on Immunohistochemistry
  and Special Stains for Computational Pathology'
abstract: Foundation models trained with self-supervised learning (SSL) on large-scale
  histological images have significantly accelerated the development of computational
  pathology. These models can serve as backbones for region-of-interest (ROI) image
  analysis or patch-level feature extractors in whole-slide images (WSIs) based on
  multiple instance learning (MIL). Existing pathology foundation models (PFMs) are
  typically pre-trained on Hematoxylin-Eosin (H&E) stained pathology images. However,
  images such as immunohistochemistry (IHC) and special stains are also frequently
  used in clinical practice. PFMs pre-trained mainly on H&E-stained images may be
  limited in clinical applications involving these non-H&E images. To address this
  issue, we propose StainNet, a a collection of self-supervised foundation models
  specifically trained for IHC and special stains in pathology images based on the
  vision transformer (ViT) architecture. StainNet contains a ViT-Small and a ViT-Base
  model, both of which are trained using a self-distillation SSL approach on over
  1.4 million patch images extracted from 20,231 publicly available IHC and special
  staining WSIs in the HISTAI database. To evaluate StainNet models, we conduct experiments
  on three in-house slide-level IHC classification tasks, three in-house ROI-level
  special stain and two public ROI-level IHC classification tasks to demonstrate their
  strong ability. We also perform ablation studies such as few-ratio learning and
  retrieval evaluations, and compare StainNet models with recent larger PFMs to further
  highlight their strengths.
